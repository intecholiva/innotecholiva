Optimizing Olive Oil Extraction Efficiency Using Horizontal Separators

This scientific study examines how different designs of horizontal separators affect the extraction efficiency of virgin olive oil, specifically from the Arbequina variety. Researchers compared decanter technologies to evaluate their impact on oil yield, moisture content, and chemical composition. Results highlight that certain separator configurations significantly improve extraction outcomes, offering producers a practical route to enhance both quality and sustainability. The study supports equipment optimization as a key factor in process innovation.

Processing Factors Affecting Alcohols and Alkyl Esters Balance in Arbequina Olive Oil

This study investigates how different processing parameters during the separation and clarification stages impact the balance of alcohols and alkyl esters in virgin olive oil extracted from the Arbequina variety. It highlights that specific conditions in these steps can influence the chemical profile of the oil, affecting both its quality and sensory attributes. The findings offer producers important guidelines to optimize processing for better flavor and stability.

Key Production Factors in High-Quality Olive Oils

This article explores the technical and operational factors that influence the quality of extra virgin olive oil. Key practices include proper olive reception and storage, appropriate crushing techniques to preserve volatile and phenolic compounds, and thorough cleaning at every stage of the process. The role of the mill master is emphasized as essential — their technical knowledge and management skills are critical for producing high-quality oils.
